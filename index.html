The Trip

14-Day Sustainable Family Train Holiday

For Dutch families who want adventure, comfort and less stress. We arrange the train routes, accommodation and family-friendly activities.

Our 14-day sustainable family train holiday is designed for Dutch families with children aged 6–11 who want adventure, comfort and less stress. Instead of spending hours planning routes, finding child-friendly accommodation and checking transport options, families receive a complete travel experience that is already organised.

The journey takes families by train to beautiful destinations in Southern Europe, such as Italy, Slovenia or Croatia. The trip combines nature and culture, so children can explore mountains, rivers, small towns and cities in one holiday. Families stay in equipped tents or mobile homes, which means there is no need to bring camping gear.

The package includes pre-booked family-friendly train routes, comfortable accommodation, a digital itinerary and curated activities. Examples of activities are mountain biking, canoeing, river swimming, climbing parks and cultural city visits. This makes the trip active and educational, but still manageable for parents.

This holiday is ideal for families who want to travel more sustainably without losing comfort. Travelling by train creates a slower and more meaningful experience. Children can enjoy the journey itself, while parents know that the main travel details have already been arranged.

Why Train Travel Is the Perfect Family Adventure in Europe

A short blog for parents who want a more meaningful, relaxed and sustainable family holiday.

Train travel makes the journey part of the holiday, with views of landscapes, mountains and nature along the way.

Camping gives families the feeling of outdoor adventure, while still offering comfort and a calm place to stay.

A Different Way to Start a Holiday

When people think about a family holiday, they often think about airports, suitcases, waiting times and planning stress. However, there is another way to start a trip: #TrainTravel. It can be slow, simple and sustainable, especially for families with children.

My Own Train Travel Experience

I have experienced different train journeys myself. Once, I travelled to the other side of the Netherlands by intercity train. The trip took quite long, mainly because it was not a high-speed train. I have also travelled to Düsseldorf by fast train, and that experience was much better. The seats were comfortable, I arrived quickly, I could sleep for a while, and I had a nice view from the window. I remember seeing fields, towns and roads passing by while hearing the soft rhythm of the train. It felt calm, comfortable and easy.

The Journey Becomes Part of the Holiday

That feeling is important for #TrainTravelWithKids. Children do not only want to arrive somewhere; they also want something to see and experience on the way. The train can become the first chapter of the holiday. This metaphor shows that the adventure already starts before the family reaches the campsite or city.

Comfort and Camping for Families

For #FamilyTravel, comfort is very important. I have been to France a few times and stayed at a campsite in a mobile home. I liked that because it still felt like camping, but it was easier than bringing all our own gear. There were activities such as swimming, playgrounds and discos, which made the holiday fun for children and less stressful for parents. That is why #Camping can work well when the accommodation is already prepared.

Why This Holiday Benefits Families

This type of holiday is beneficial because it gives families safety, comfort and shared memories. Parents save time because the route, accommodation and activities are already planned. Children get variety: one day they can explore nature, the next day they can visit a town or swim in a lake. Not only does this make the trip easier for parents, but it also gives children a more active and educational experience.

Adventure in Southern Europe

Our concept focuses on #SouthernEurope, especially Italy, Slovenia and Croatia. These countries fit an #AdventurousHoliday because they offer nature, culture and activities. Families can go #Hiking in the mountains, visit small towns, swim in rivers or lakes and discover local food. This combination makes the trip interesting for both adults and children.

A Safe, Fun and Responsible Trip

What I personally like is the balance. It is not only about adventure, not only about comfort and not only about sustainability. It is about all three: a safe trip, a fun trip and a responsible trip. This list of three helps to emphasise the main point. It also shows that a family holiday can be exciting without becoming chaotic.

Ready to Travel Differently?

In the end, #Backpacking, camping and train travel do not have to be only for young solo travellers. With the right planning, travelling with kids through Italy, Slovenia or Croatia can be practical, meaningful and memorable.

Are you ready to make the journey part of the memory?

Who We Are

Family Tracks Europe is a travel company created for Dutch families who want to explore Europe in a more relaxed, adventurous and responsible way. Our concept focuses on 14-day train holidays to Southern Europe, especially Italy, Slovenia and Croatia. We design trips for families with children aged 6–11 who want nature, culture and comfort, but do not want the stress of organising everything themselves.

We believe that a family holiday should not only be about arriving at a destination. The journey itself can also become part of the experience. By travelling by train, staying in family-friendly accommodation and choosing activities that connect families with local places, we want to make holidays more meaningful.

Our Mission

Our mission is to make sustainable family travel easier and more attractive. Many families want to make better choices, but they also want safety, comfort and clear planning. That is why we offer a complete concept with pre-planned train routes, ready-to-use camping accommodation and selected family activities.

We want parents to feel supported and children to feel excited. Families should be able to enjoy the view from the train, discover small towns, hike in nature, swim in lakes or rivers and experience local culture without feeling overwhelmed by planning.

Our CSR Policy

Our CSR policy is based on three main values: people, planet and profit. For people, we focus on families, local communities and partners. For the planet, we reduce the need for flying by offering train-based travel. For profit, we want tourism income to support local destinations instead of only using them as a background for tourism.

A concrete sustainable action is that our holidays are built around train travel instead of short-haul flights. Another action is that we work with local campsites, activity providers and small-scale partners where possible. This helps reduce import leakage and supports the multiplier effect, because more money stays in the destination. We also encourage slower travel. Families stay longer in selected areas instead of rushing from one place to another. This reduces pressure on destinations and gives families more time to understand the local culture and natural environment. Click here.

Our CSR USP

Our CSR USP is simple: families can travel more responsibly without losing comfort, safety or adventure. We combine train-based routes, ready-to-use camping accommodation, local partners, slower travel and honest communication. This makes sustainable travel practical for parents and exciting for children.

Our Stakeholders

Sustainable tourism is not something we can do alone. It requires collaboration with different stakeholders. Our internal stakeholders are our travel planners, customer service team and content creators. They design realistic routes, check family-friendly options and communicate honestly.

Our external stakeholders include families, train operators, campsites, mobile home providers, local activity companies, nature parks and local communities. Train operators help us make the journey practical. Campsites and accommodation partners provide comfort. Local activity providers offer experiences such as hiking, cycling, swimming and cultural visits. Local communities are important because tourism should respect their daily life and culture.

Avoiding Greenwashing

We do not claim that our holidays are perfect or 100% impact-free. Every trip has an environmental footprint. However, we do believe that families can make better choices. We avoid greenwashing by being specific about what we do: we use train routes, promote longer stays, work with local partners and give families clear information.

We also avoid exaggerated language. Instead of saying “completely green” or “zero impact”, we explain the real choices behind the trip. This makes our communication more honest and believable.

Our Vision for the Future

Our vision is to become a trusted family travel brand for sustainable train holidays in Europe. In the future, we want to expand our routes to more destinations and work with more certified accommodation and activity partners. We also want to add optional regenerative activities, such as supporting local nature projects or community-based initiatives.

For us, responsible travel is not about making families feel guilty. It is about giving them easy and enjoyable choices. A better holiday should still feel fun, comfortable and adventurous. With Family Tracks Europe, families can travel slower, connect deeper and create memories that last longer than the trip itself.
